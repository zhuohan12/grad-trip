01
Hurricane Ridge · Sol Duc Falls + Hot Springs · Lake Crescent
Tuesday, Aug 4 · Subalpine ecosystem
7:30 AM
Hurricane Ridge
Main hike
Drive 17 miles south from Port Angeles (~45 min) to the most accessible subalpine zone in the park at 5,242 ft. Olympic marmots graze the meadows and the full Olympic Range fills the horizon. Hit the trailhead early — parking is completely full by 10 AM in August.
Hurricane Hill Trail  3.2 mi RT  ·  700 ft gain  ·  ~2.5 hrs  ·  Moderate
Big Meadow loop  0.5 mi paved  ·  Easy add-on near visitor centre
1:30 PM
Sol Duc Falls
45-min drive west on Hwy 101, then 12 miles in off the highway. An easy 1.6 mi RT walk through old-growth Douglas fir leads to three-chute falls plunging into a narrow basalt gorge.
1.6 mi RT  ·  ~1.5 hrs  ·  Easy
3:30 PM
Sol Duc Hot Springs
Three mineral pools (98°F, 101°F, 104°F) at the Sol Duc Hot Springs Resort, two miles back down the road from the falls trailhead. Day-use pass ~$20, towel rental available. The perfect recovery for the legs after Hurricane Hill. Plan ~1 hr.
Day-use pass  ~$20  ·  Bring swimsuit + towel (or rent)
5:00 PM
Lake Crescent + Marymere Falls
Glacially carved lake of electric blue-green, impossibly clear. Marymere Falls Trail (1.8 mi RT) begins right from the lakeside parking and leads through cathedral old-growth to a 90-ft waterfall. Dinner at Storm King Smokehouse on the way back.
1.8 mi RT  ·  ~1.5 hrs  ·  Easy–moderate
02
Hoh Rainforest · Ruby Beach · Rialto Beach
Wednesday, Aug 5 · Rainforest + Pacific Coast ecosystems
6:30 AM
Depart Port Angeles
Leave no later than 6:30 AM. The Hoh is 2 hours away and a ranger checkpoint 5 miles before the visitor centre creates 2+ hour backlogs after 11 AM in peak summer. A timed-entry permit may also be required — check nps.gov/olym before the trip and book if needed.
Rainforest ecosystem begins
9:00 AM
Hoh Rainforest
Main hike
One of the few temperate rainforests on Earth. Start with the Hall of Mosses (0.8 mi loop) — bigleaf maple hung with club moss, entirely otherworldly. Then push 2–3 miles into the Hoh River Trail for old-growth solitude: Sitka spruce over 300 ft, complete quiet except for the river. The biodiversity in every square metre is staggering.
Hall of Mosses  0.8 mi loop
Hoh River Trail  2–3 mi in and back
~2.5 hrs total  ·  Easy
Pacific Coast ecosystem begins
1:00 PM
Ruby Beach
50-min drive south from Hoh on Hwy 101. Layered sea stacks, Abbey Island, red-purple pebbles from garnet-heavy volcanic rock. Five-minute trail from parking. Linger 2 hrs.
4:30 PM
Rialto Beach
Main hike Check tide tables first
~1.5 hr drive north via Forks. Hike to Hole-in-the-Wall, 3 mi RT along cobblestone coast flanked by bleached driftwood and sea stacks. Natural arch only passable at low tide — check the La Push tide table at tidesandcurrents.noaa.gov. August sunset ~8:30 PM, so you'll catch golden hour and sunset here. Then 1.5 hrs back to Port Angeles.
3 mi RT  ·  ~2.5 hrs  ·  Easy
Field notes & logistics
Entry pass — America the Beautiful pass ($80/yr) covers Olympic NP + all US national parks. Buy at the entrance gate or online.
Hoh timed entry — May be required in peak summer. Check nps.gov/olym before the trip and book in advance if available.
Rialto tides — Look up Aug 5 low tide for La Push at tidesandcurrents.noaa.gov. Plan to be at the arch within 2 hrs of low tide.
Stargazing access — Call the Hurricane Ridge visitor centre (+1 360-565-3130) when you arrive to confirm whether the road is open on the night of Aug 3.
Gear essentials — Waterproof trail shoes (Hoh is muddy year-round), warm jacket + beanie for the ridge at night, trekking poles optional for Hurricane Hill.
Wildlife — Olympic black bears are active in summer. Store food properly. Mountain goats roam Hurricane Ridge — keep 50 yards distance.
