01
Downtown Banff · Moraine Lake · Lake Louise · Little Beehive
Saturday, July 25 · Glacial lakes
8:30 AM
Morning in downtown Banff
Drive in from Canmore (~25 min) for a relaxed start — coffee on Banff Avenue and a quick look at the Bow River before the lakes. Keep it short: you need to be at the Lake Louise Park & Ride for your shuttle window, ~45 min further west. Leave Banff by ~11:30 AM.
Moraine Lake · shuttle access only
1:00 PM
Moraine Lake
Easy walk Shuttle reservation required
Board the 1 PM Parks Canada shuttle at the Lake Louise Park & Ride (private vehicles can't drive Moraine Lake Road). Climb the Rockpile for the classic Ten Peaks view, then walk the flat Lakeshore Trail along the water. Aim to keep it to about an hour so you can catch the connector with time to spare.
Rockpile + Lakeshore Trail  ~3 km total  ·  flat  ·  ~1 hr  ·  Easy
2:30 PM
Lake Connector to Lake Louise
Take the free Lake Connector shuttle between the two lakes (included with your reservation, first come first served). Allow for a short wait — it's the least predictable link in the day. Arrive at the Lake Louise lakeshore around 3:00–3:15.
3:15 PM
Little Beehive
Main hike Last shuttle ~7:30 PM
Climb from the Lake Louise lakeshore past Mirror Lake to the Lake Agnes Tea House, then up the short spur to the Little Beehive lookout over the Bow Valley. This is the version of the climb that fits before the last shuttle — Big Beehive adds ~1.5 hrs and would push you past it. If the connector ran late, turn around at the tea house and you'll still have done most of the climb. Back at the lakeshore by ~6:45.
Little Beehive via Lake Agnes  ~8.5 km RT  ·  ~500 m gain  ·  ~3–3.5 hrs  ·  Moderate
Back toward Banff
7:30 PM
Dinner in downtown Banff
Catch a return shuttle to the Park & Ride (last one ~7:30 PM), then drive back east to Banff townsite for dinner on Banff Avenue. From there it's ~25 min home to Canmore.
10:00 PM
Return to Canmore
~25 min east on the Trans-Canada to 160 Kananaskis Way. Watch for elk on the highway after dark.
02
Emerald Lake canoe · Natural Bridge · Wapta Falls · Takakkaw Falls
Sunday, July 26 · Yoho National Park (BC)
7:45 AM
Depart Canmore
Early start is the whole strategy today — it gets you to Emerald Lake before the parking fills and while the water is still glassy. ~1.25 hrs west on the Trans-Canada, over the BC border past Field. Weekend crowds make the early arrival matter even more.
Emerald Lake · paddle first
9:15 AM
Emerald Lake — canoe
Canoe First come, no reservations
Rent at the Boathouse Trading Co. and paddle the green glacial water under the President Range. Going first thing means easy parking, calm water, and short or no rental line. One adult (19+) per canoe required.
Canoe rental  $100/hr per canoe (fits up to 3)  ·  last canoes out 4:45 PM
10:45 AM
Natural Bridge
A quick stop on Emerald Lake Road as you head back out — the Kicking Horse River has carved a rock bridge and is steadily punching through it. Five minutes of walking, a couple of viewpoints. Still mid-morning, so ahead of the day's peak.
Viewpoint  ·  ~15 min
Wapta Falls · western end
11:45 AM
Wapta Falls
Short hike
Drive ~30 min west to the widest waterfall on the Kicking Horse River. The hike in keeps crowds thinner than the roadside stops, so midday timing is fine here. Doing Wapta now — before doubling back east — is what keeps the day from backtracking.
Wapta Falls Trail  4.6 km RT  ·  ~150 m gain  ·  ~1.5–2 hrs  ·  Easy–moderate
Takakkaw Falls · on the way home
3:00 PM
Takakkaw Falls
Tight switchbacks
Drive back east and up Yoho Valley Road to one of Canada's tallest waterfalls (~373 m). It's the easternmost stop, closest to home, so it's the natural finale. Busier in the afternoon, but it's a giant falls where crowds matter far less than at the canoe dock. Short walk from the lot to the base.
Yoho Valley Road  ·  no long trailers  ·  ~45 min on site
4:30 PM
Return to Canmore
~1.5 hrs back east on the Trans-Canada to 160 Kananaskis Way. Dinner in Canmore on Main Street.
03
Johnston Canyon · Lake Minnewanka · on to Calgary
Monday, July 27 · Canyon, lakeshore, city
7:30 AM
Depart Canmore
Early start beats the Johnston Canyon crowds, which build fast even on a weekday. ~45 min to the trailhead on the Bow Valley Parkway (Hwy 1A). Pack the car fully — you're not returning to Canmore, you finish in Calgary tonight.
Johnston Canyon · go early
8:15 AM
Johnston Canyon
Main hike Crowds build by mid-morning
Walk the catwalks bolted into the limestone canyon to the Lower then Upper Falls. Going at opening keeps the narrow walkways from bottlenecking. Monday helps too, but the lot still fills — early is the move. Turn around at the Lower Falls if you're short on time; the first section is the most dramatic.
Lower + Upper Falls  ~5.4 km RT  ·  ~120 m gain  ·  ~2.5 hrs  ·  Easy–moderate
Lake Minnewanka · east side, toward Calgary
11:30 AM
Lake Minnewanka loop
Easy strolls
Drive the scenic loop on the east side of the park — Two Jack Lake for the mirror reflections, then Lake Minnewanka itself for an easy lakeshore wander. Optional 1-hour boat cruise if the timing lines up. Being on the east side means you're already pointed toward Calgary.
Lakeshore walks  ·  ~1.5–2 hrs  ·  Easy  ·  optional boat cruise
1:30 PM
Lunch in Banff townsite
Loop back into Banff (~15 min) for a late lunch on Banff Avenue before leaving the mountains. Last chance for park-town food and a final look at the peaks.
Off to the city
3:00 PM
Drive to Calgary
~1.5 hrs east on the Trans-Canada to 105B Magnolia Grove SE. Leaving mid-afternoon gets you in with daylight to spare for your friend to explore — Stephen Avenue, the Calgary Tower, Prince's Island Park, or the East Village riverwalk are all easy evening wanders.
Field notes & logistics
Park passes — Banff, Yoho, and the lakes corridor all need a valid Parks Canada pass. A Discovery Pass covers the whole trip; display it on the dash. Note: the Canada Strong Pass waived Banff entry fees June 19–Sep 7 in 2026 — verify before you go.
Moraine shuttle — Reserve the 1 PM slot in advance at reservation.pc.gc.ca; Moraine Lake Road is closed to private cars. 40% of seats release in April, the rest 48 hrs before at 8 AM MT. The free Lake Connector is first come, first served.
Last shuttle — The final return from the Lake Louise lakeshore to the Park & Ride is ~7:30 PM. It's the hard deadline that shapes Day 1 — keep Little Beehive moving to make it.
Emerald Lake canoe — First come, no reservations; $100/hr per canoe, last boats out 4:45 PM. Arrive before 10 AM for parking and calm water. No cell service at the lake.
Yoho Valley Road — The road to Takakkaw Falls opens late June and has tight switchbacks — no long trailers or large RVs. Confirm it's open for late July before relying on it.
Wildlife + weather — Elk, deer, black and grizzly bears are active in July. Carry bear spray on the trails, keep distance, and slow down at dusk on the highways. Mountain weather shifts fast; pack a warm layer and rain shell even on clear mornings.
