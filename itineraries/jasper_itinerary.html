Drive in · Medicine Lake · Maligne Lake · Jasper at night
Wednesday, July 22 · Arrival + glacial lakes
7:00 AM
Depart DoubleTree by Hilton Kamloops
Yellowhead Hwy 5 north to Tête Jaune Cache, then Hwy 16 east. ~5.5 hrs of pure driving, so an early start keeps the afternoon open for the lakes. Fill up before Valemount — fuel thins out past there.
12:30 PM
Mount Robson Welcome Centre
Lunch stop with the clearest roadside view of the highest peak in the Canadian Rockies (3,954 m). Overlander Falls Trail (1 km RT) is right next door if you want to stretch. Gas + café on site.
Maligne Valley · Glacial lakes
2:30 PM
Medicine Lake
Turn onto Maligne Lake Road just east of Jasper townsite. Medicine Lake is the "disappearing lake" — it drains through an underground karst system, so its level swings dramatically through the season. Quick photo stop, scan the shoreline for elk and bears.
Roadside viewpoint  ·  ~20 min
3:30 PM
Maligne Lake
Easy walk
The end of the road, and the largest natural lake in the Rockies — vivid turquoise framed by glaciated peaks. Arriving mid-afternoon, treat this as a scenic wind-down: walk the Mary Schäffer Loop (3.2 km, flat, ~1 hr) along the shoreline. The Spirit Island boat cruise and the Bald Hills hike both need more time than you'll have today — save those for a future trip or a full Maligne day.

Back through the townsite
6:30 PM
Downtown Jasper — Connaught Drive
Drive back down Maligne Lake Road and into the townsite for dinner. Jasper Brewing Co. on Connaught Drive does local craft beer and solid pub food (open till midnight); Evil Dave's Grill is the sit-down option. Wander Patricia Street for shops and ice cream. A relaxed evening to close the long drive day.
8:30 PM
Drive to Hinton
~50 min east on Hwy 16 to 130 Nickerson Ave. Watch for elk and deer on the highway at dusk — this stretch is an active wildlife corridor, so keep your speed down after dark.
02
Sulphur Skyline · Miette Hot Springs · Pyramid Lake
Thursday, July 23 · Alpine ridge + soak
7:00 AM
Depart Hinton
~45 min west on Hwy 16, then 17 km up the Miette Hot Springs Road spur. The spur branches off before you reach the townsite, so there's zero backtracking. Start the hike early — Sulphur Skyline bakes in afternoon sun and the small lot fills fast.
Fiddle Valley · Sulphur ridge
8:30 AM
Sulphur Skyline Trail
Main hike Steep · strenuous
Trailhead is at the Miette Hot Springs parking lot. A relentless climb on a well-graded path to a bare summit at 2,070 m with a full 360° panorama over the Fiddle Valley and the Ashlar Ridge. One of the best effort-to-reward viewpoints in the park — but the gain is no joke, so pace yourself and carry 2L+ of water.

Wheatless Wanderlust note: They flag Sulphur Skyline as one of Jasper's premier but genuinely tough hikes, with roughly 4.9 mi / 2,142 ft of climbing and a 360-degree summit payoff. Treat the hot springs as recovery, not an optional luxury. Photos + guide
12:30 PM
Miette Hot Springs
Recovery soak
Walk straight off the trail into the hottest natural mineral springs in the Canadian Rockies (pools kept around 40°C). The perfect reward for the legs. Day pass ~$20 CAD, swimsuit + towel needed (rentals available). Grab lunch at the springs café or picnic in the lot afterward.
Day pass  ~$20 CAD  ·  ~1.5 hrs incl. lunch
Montane lakes near the townsite
3:30 PM
Pyramid Lake + Pyramid Island
Drive back west past the townsite and 6 km up Pyramid Lake Road. Cross the footbridge to Pyramid Island — a tiny pine-covered islet with Pyramid Mountain reflected in the water. Ridiculously photogenic for almost no effort. Rent a canoe if the legs have recovered, or just take the easy loop trail.
Footbridge + island  ·  ~1.5 hrs  ·  Easy
Wheatless Wanderlust note: Their Jasper plan highlights Pyramid Island's wooden footbridge as the quick, high-reward stop here; keep this easy unless the group specifically wants to rent a boat from the lodge area. Photos + guide
5:30 PM
Back to Hinton
~50 min east on Hwy 16 to 130 Nickerson Ave. Dinner in town, or detour through the Jasper townsite one more time if you want a final meal in the park.
Day 2 is front-loaded by design.  Sulphur Skyline's 700 m climb is the hardest single effort of the trip, so it goes first while you're fresh, with the hot springs as the built-in reward right at the trailhead. Pyramid Lake in the afternoon is deliberately low-effort. If anyone's knees are done after the ridge, drop Pyramid and head straight back — you won't be missing a hike, just a photo stop.
Field notes & logistics
Park pass — Both days are inside Jasper National Park. A Parks Canada Discovery Pass or daily entry pass is required; display it on the dash. Buy online at parks.canada.ca or at the East Gate on Hwy 16.
Hinton ↔ Jasper — Your base adds ~50 min each way. Both days are built to absorb it: Day 1 stops are on the drive in, Day 2's Miette spur comes before the townsite.
Miette Hot Springs season — Typically open early May to mid-October. Confirm 2026 dates and hours at parks.canada.ca before relying on the soak.
Maligne Lake timing — Arriving ~3:30 PM means the easy lakeshore loop, not the Spirit Island cruise (last sailings are usually late afternoon and book out). Reserve ahead if you decide you want it.
Wildlife — Elk, deer, black + grizzly bears all active in July. Carry bear spray on Sulphur Skyline, keep distance, and drive slowly at dawn/dusk on Hwy 16.
Gear — Layers for the ridge (summit is windy + cool), 2L+ water, sun protection, swimsuit + quick-dry towel for Miette, bug spray for the valley.
