01
Calgary to Whitefish · the Going-to-the-Sun Road
Tuesday, Jul 28 · Travel day via the east entrance
7:30 AM
Depart Calgary — head for the east side
Rather than the direct western route to Whitefish, go south through the Alberta plains toward the Chief Mountain crossing (~2.75–3 hrs via Hwy 2, Hwy 5 past Cardston, then the Chief Mountain Hwy / Hwy 6). This brings you to the east end of the Going-to-the-Sun Road so you can drive the whole thing on the way to base. Glacier sits in Mountain Time, same as Calgary — no clock change today. Fuel up before Cardston.
~10:30 AM
Chief Mountain border crossing
Passports + US entry authorization
A seasonal, high-alpine crossing on the edge of Waterton — one of the most scenic ports of entry in the country, open 7 AM–10 PM in summer. Have everyone's passport and US entry authorization (ESTA / visa under the Visa Waiver Program) ready, and declare any fresh food. Don't carry bear spray across — buy it on the US side. From here it's ~45 min down to St Mary.
~11:30 AM
St Mary · east entrance + lunch
The eastern gateway to the park. Grab lunch and top up fuel in St Mary (there's no fuel on the road ahead), and have your park pass ready for the entrance station. This is where the Going-to-the-Sun Road begins.
Going-to-the-Sun Road · east to west
~12:15 PM
Drive the Going-to-the-Sun Road
Scenic drive Slow, winding · 21-ft vehicle limit
The dramatic east half first: along St Mary Lake to the Wild Goose Island overlook, then the Jackson Glacier Overlook — the one glacier you can see from the road — climbing to Logan Pass at the Continental Divide. Parking at the pass is capped at 3 hrs and often full midday, but you're just passing through (you'll be back tomorrow), so roll on if it's packed. Then the dizzying west descent past the Weeping Wall, Bird Woman Falls, and the Loop down to Lake McDonald. Budget ~3 hrs with stops; it's a slow 40-mph road and your SUV is well under the 21-ft limit.
St Mary → West Glacier  ~50 mi  ·  ~2 hrs driving, ~3 hrs with viewpoints
~3:30 PM
West Glacier · supply stop
Off the road's west end. Stock up before the hiking days: bear spray (outdoor shops in Columbia Falls/Whitefish, or REI in Kalispell), groceries, and plenty of water. Bear spray can't fly, so this is your one window to buy it for the trip.
~4:30 PM
Check in · Whitefish
~40 min from West Glacier to 902 Karrow Ave — your base for all three nights. Drop bags, then walk Central Avenue for dinner. Optional sunset at City Beach on Whitefish Lake. Make it an early night: tomorrow's shuttle and Highline are a long, full day.
02
Going-to-the-Sun Road · Logan Pass · Highline Trail
Wednesday, Jul 29 · Alpine traverse along the Garden Wall
8:00 AM
Slow start, depart Whitefish
Shuttle ticket valid 10 AM onward
No rush this morning — your shuttle isn't valid until 10 AM, so a relaxed breakfast and an 8 AM departure gets you to the West Glacier / Apgar entrance (~45 min) with time to spare. Park at the Apgar transit hub; private cars can't be left at Logan Pass for a full-day hike under the 2026 three-hour limit, which is why the hike runs off the shuttle.
~8:45 AM
Lake McDonald · short leg-loosener
Fill the wait before your shuttle window with something flat and easy near Apgar: the coloured pebbles along the Lake McDonald shoreline, or the Trail of the Cedars loop (~1 mi) at Avalanche. Keep it short — save the legs for Highline. Back to the Apgar shuttle stop by ~9:45.
Going-to-the-Sun Road · ride to the pass
10:00 AM
Shuttle up the Going-to-the-Sun Road
Scenic drive Ticketed · 10 AM
Board at 10 AM, the start of your ticket window. The shuttle climbs the full western half of the Going-to-the-Sun Road — past Lake McDonald, up through the cliffs of the Garden Wall, over the Loop switchback to Logan Pass. Riding up is the road experience without anyone white-knuckling the wheel, and it solves the parking problem at the top. In 2026 the west-side route runs express (Apgar and Lake McDonald Lodge only, then Logan Pass), so budget ~1.5 hrs to the pass. The last downhill shuttle leaves Logan Pass at 7:30 PM and stops at The Loop, so your finish time has plenty of room.
Highline Trail · the Garden Wall traverse
~11:30 AM
Logan Pass → Highline Trail
Main hike Exposed ledge near the start
From Logan Pass (6,646 ft) the trail begins with the famous narrow ledge below the Garden Wall — a hand cable bolted to the rock, a big drop to the road below, but wide enough and safe at a steady pace. It then undulates through alpine meadow to Haystack Pass (the main climb, ~3.5 mi), continues to Granite Park Chalet at 7.6 mi, then drops ~4 mi of switchbacks to The Loop. Mountain goats and bighorn sheep are common along the wall. An ~11:30 start has you finishing around 5:30 — well ahead of the 7:30 PM last shuttle — so the pace is relaxed, not rushed.
Highline · Logan Pass to The Loop  11.6 mi / 18 km point-to-point  ·  ~1,450 ft / 440 m gain (net descent)  ·  ~6 hrs  ·  Moderate–strenuous
Grinnell Glacier Overlook spur  optional, steep  ·  +1.2 mi / +1,000 ft RT  ·  skip it — you're seeing the glacier up close tomorrow
~5:30 PM
The Loop → shuttle back
Shuttle pickup
Catch a downhill shuttle at The Loop back to Apgar, where the car is. Afternoon and evening shuttles stop here for descending Highline hikers; the last one off Logan Pass leaves at 7:30 PM and passes The Loop shortly after, so a ~5:30 arrival means a short wait, not a scramble. Just don't dawdle past the last bus — there's no other ride back to your car.
~6:30 PM
Drive back to Whitefish
~45 min west to base. Later dinner on Central Avenue tonight given the shifted timing. Rest the legs — tomorrow is the bigger climb, and it starts very early.
03
Many Glacier · Grinnell Glacier Trail
Thursday, Jul 30 · Turquoise lakes and the glacier basin
5:30 AM
Depart Whitefish — early
Many Glacier is ~2.5–3 hrs away on the east side, via US-2 to East Glacier, then MT-49 and US-89 north to Babb, then Many Glacier Road in. The Going-to-the-Sun Road does not connect to Many Glacier — this long loop around the south end is the only road in, so the early start is unavoidable.
Many Glacier · the east side
~8:30 AM
Grinnell Glacier Trailhead
Lot fills mid-morning
Park between the Many Glacier Hotel and the Swiftcurrent Motor Inn. The 2025 construction is finished and parking expanded to 339 spaces for 2026, but the lot still fills by mid-morning on weekends — aim to arrive by 8:30. If it's full, use the larger Many Glacier Hotel lot and pick up the connector trail along Swiftcurrent Lake.
~9:00 AM
Grinnell Glacier Trail
Main hike Grizzly habitat — carry spray
A flat 2 mi warm-up along Swiftcurrent Lake and Lake Josephine, then 2.5 mi of real climbing past a trailside waterfall (you walk under it — stash electronics), then a steep final mile of rocky steps to the glacier basin: a blue-green meltwater pool beneath the Garden Wall and the Continental Divide. Prime bear country, so make noise and keep the spray accessible. A $35 boat shuttle across the two lakes can trim ~3.5 mi off the round trip if anyone's legs are spent from Highline.
Grinnell Glacier · out-and-back  11 mi / 18 km RT  ·  2,600 ft / 790 m gain  ·  ~6–7 hrs  ·  Strenuous
~1:00 PM
Lunch at the glacier basin
Refuel at the top with the glacier and falls in front of you — there's a pit toilet and benches near the final switchback just below. Layer up: the wind coming off the ice is cold once you stop moving and the climb-sweat cools. Turn around when you're ready; it's the same trail back down.
~5:30 PM
Drive back to Whitefish
~2.5–3 hrs back around to base. Dinner in Whitefish, then pack tonight — tomorrow you'll slip back into the park for a relaxed morning at Lake McDonald before the easy drive west to Spokane (checkout 11 AM).
04
A last Glacier morning, then west to Spokane
Friday, Jul 31 · Half day in the park + ~254 mi / ~4.5 hrs to Spokane
7:30 AM
Early breakfast, load the car
Spokane is a much shorter day than the old Seattle push — only ~254 mi / ~4.5 hrs via US-2 — so there's no rush and time for one more walk in the park. Pack up, grab breakfast in Whitefish, and don't worry about the 11 AM checkout; you'll be long gone. Top up fuel on the way out.
One last morning in Glacier · Lake McDonald
~8:15 AM
Apgar & Lake McDonald
Easy stroll
~35 min east to West Glacier and the foot of Lake McDonald — the park's biggest lake, ringed by peaks and famous for its multicoloured pebble shoreline at Apgar. It's a gentle bookend after two hard hiking days: wander the beach, rent nothing, just take it in with a coffee. If the light's good the reflections off the still morning water are the best photo of the whole leg.
Apgar shoreline  flat, a few min from parking  ·  as long as you like
~8:45 AM
Trail of the Cedars + Avalanche (optional)
Optional add-on
If legs are willing, drive ~25 min up the Going-to-the-Sun Road to the Trail of the Cedars, an easy 1 mi boardwalk loop through old-growth red cedar and hemlock — flat, shaded, and a completely different Glacier from the alpine you've had. Those wanting one more real hike can continue from the same trailhead to Avalanche Lake (4.5 mi RT, ~600 ft, ~2.5 hrs) beneath a wall of waterfalls. Skip either without guilt to keep the drive easy.
Trail of the Cedars  1 mi loop  ·  Easy
Avalanche Lake  4.5 mi RT  ·  ~600 ft  ·  Moderate
West on US-2 · Montana → Idaho → Washington
~11:00 AM
Back through Whitefish, pick up US-2 west
Roll back past Whitefish and settle onto US-2 heading west through Libby and the Kootenai River country, then across the Idaho panhandle. It's a two-lane highway rather than interstate but scenic and quiet. You gain an hour crossing into Pacific time at the Idaho line, so the clock stays kind. Whitefish → Spokane is only ~254 mi even with the park detour behind you.
~1:00 PM PT
Sandpoint, ID · lakeside lunch
The natural break: a pretty resort town on the shore of Lake Pend Oreille, right on the route. Waterfront cafés at City Beach, easy parking, a good stretch of the legs. (Coeur d'Alene is the alternative if you'd rather dip south toward the end of the drive.)
~4:00–5:00 PM PT
Arrive Spokane
417 West Barnes Road, on the north side of the city — your base for the night. Even with a full morning in Glacier and the lunch stop, the hour you gain lands you in by late afternoon. Grab groceries and an early dinner; tomorrow is the long, scenic drive over Chinook Pass to Mount Rainier's Sunrise side, so an early night helps.
Two big days back to back — but a gentler exit than before.  Highline (18 km) and Grinnell (18 km, 2,600 ft) on consecutive days is a lot, especially after the Calgary drive in. The good news: routing out to Spokane instead of Seattle turns the old ~8.5 hr slog into an easy ~4.5 hr afternoon, which is exactly why there's room for a relaxed Glacier morning on Jul 31. Keep that morning light (Lake McDonald, maybe Trail of the Cedars) if the legs are cooked — save Avalanche Lake for those still keen. Note the Aug 1 follow-on is itself a long, scenic haul (Spokane → Chinook Pass → Sunrise, ~5.5 hrs) before hiking, so bank the rest tonight.
Field notes & logistics
Entry pass — An America the Beautiful pass ($80/yr) covers Glacier plus Rainier and Olympic later in your trip. No vehicle reservation is required anywhere in the park in 2026 — the timed-entry pilot was dropped.
Logan Pass / Highline shuttle — Your Jul 29 ticket is valid from 10 AM, so Highline starts ~11:30 and finishes The Loop ~5:30 PM. The last downhill shuttle leaves Logan Pass at 7:30 PM (stopping at The Loop), so you've got a ~2-hour buffer. There's no other way back to your car if you miss it. West-side route is express: Apgar and Lake McDonald Lodge only.
Going-to-the-Sun Road — You drive the east half on arrival (Jul 28) and ride the west half by shuttle on hike day (Jul 29) — the full road, no repeat. The alpine section opens late June / early July depending on snow; confirm it's fully open on the NPS Glacier "Current Conditions" page beforehand. Vehicles over 21 ft are barred between Avalanche and Rising Sun.
Many Glacier access — Open and normal for 2026 (2025 Swiftcurrent construction is done). No reservation needed, but arrive before ~8:30 AM on weekends. Snow can linger on Grinnell's final pitch into early summer — fine by late July.
Bears — Both trails cross prime grizzly habitat. Carry bear spray where you can reach it, hike in a group, and make noise on blind corners. Buy spray in Montana (you can't fly with it or bring it over the border).
Gear & weather — Layers and a wind shell (frigid off the glacier and at the pass), 2L+ water each, sturdy boots, sun protection for the exposed Garden Wall, trekking poles for the Grinnell descent.
