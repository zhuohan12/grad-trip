Friday, July 24 · One of the world's great drives
7:30 AM
Set off early
There is no fuel between Saskatchewan River Crossing and the ends of the Parkway — top up before you start and again at the Crossing if you're low. Pack lunch and plenty of water; services are minimal. Aim to be at Athabasca Falls before the first tour buses.
Waterfalls · Upper Parkway
8:00 AM
Athabasca Falls
Short walk
The most powerful falls on the Parkway — not tall, but a huge volume of glacial meltwater forced through a narrow quartzite gorge. Paved walkways and bridges loop both sides. Go early; this is one of the busiest stops by mid-morning.
~1 km of paved loops  ·  ~30 min  ·  Easy
9:00 AM
Sunwapta Falls
Short walk
Glacial water curves around a small island before dropping into a tight canyon. The upper falls are steps from the lot; a quiet 2 km round-trip trail leads to the less-crowded lower falls if you want to stretch. Café on site.
Upper falls 5 min  ·  lower falls 2 km RT  ·  ~30–45 min
10:15 AM
Tangle Creek Falls
Roadside
A tiered roadside cascade right beside Hwy 93 — park across the road and shoot it in five minutes. Cross carefully; there's no crosswalk and traffic moves fast here.
Roadside  ·  ~15 min
Columbia Icefield · The high point
10:45 AM
Toe of the Athabasca Glacier Trailhead
Short hikeCold + windy
Walk up the moraine toward the snout of the Athabasca Glacier, with interpretive markers showing how far the ice has retreated decade by decade — sobering and spectacular. Stay behind the barriers; the ice is unstable and hides crevasses. The Icefield Centre across the highway has lunch and restrooms, plus the optional Ice Explorer and Skywalk if you pre-booked.
Forefield trail  ~1.8 km RT  ·  ~45 min  ·  Easy–moderate, exposed
12:00 PM
Wilcox Pass (optional)
If time + energy
If you're ahead of schedule and want one real hike, the first 30–40 min of Wilcox Pass climbs to a red-chair viewpoint straight across from the Athabasca Glacier — arguably a better glacier view than the toe itself. Full pass is 8 km RT; turn around at the meadow if you just want the view. Bighorn sheep common.
To the viewpoint  ~3 km RT  ·  ~1.5 hrs  ·  Moderate
Canyon + lakes · Lower Parkway
2:30 PM
Mistaya Canyon
Short hike
A 10-minute walk downhill from the lot leads to a narrow, twisting limestone slot carved by the Mistaya River — whirlpools, a natural arch, and foaming turquoise water. Remember the climb back up is the work. Just south of Saskatchewan River Crossing (last fuel + food).
~1 km RT  ·  ~30–40 min  ·  Short but steep return
3:45 PM
Peyto Lake
Short walk
The wolf-head-shaped lake of every postcard — an electric glacial turquoise from the rock flour suspended in the meltwater. A paved 15-min walk from the Bow Summit lot leads to the upgraded viewing platform. Best light is midday-to-afternoon, so your timing is right.
~1.2 km RT to platform  ·  ~45 min  ·  Easy
4:45 PM
Bow Lake
Lakeshore
A broad, calm lake at the foot of Bow Summit, quieter than Lake Louise and often more beautiful. Stroll the shoreline, or grab a coffee at Num-Ti-Jah Lodge's provisions counter. A gentle final stop before you leave the Parkway.
Lakeshore wander  ·  ~45 min  ·  Easy
Off the Parkway
6:00 PM
Continue to Canmore
Rejoin Hwy 93 south past Lake Louise, merge onto the Trans-Canada Hwy 1 east, and run down to 160 Kananaskis Way, Canmore. From Bow Lake it's roughly 1.5 hrs without stops (~140 km). Fuel up at Lake Louise village if you're low, and watch for wildlife at dusk on the Trans-Canada.
This is a stop-heavy day, not a hiking day.  Eight or nine pull-offs across ~230 km means the clock is your real constraint, not distance. The schedule front-loads the must-sees and treats Wilcox Pass as a pressure valve: skip it without guilt if you're behind. If you'd rather hike more and stop less, drop Tangle Creek and one of the falls and put that time into Wilcox.
Field notes & logistics
Park pass — The Parkway runs through Jasper + Banff National Parks; a valid Parks Canada pass is required and checked. Display it on the dash.
Fuel — Only reliable fuel is at the ends + Saskatchewan River Crossing. Start full; top up at the Crossing.
Icefield add-ons — The Ice Explorer + Skywalk sell out; pre-book online if you want them. They add ~2 hrs, so cut other stops if so.
Weather — The Icefield section is high and cold even in July; carry a warm layer + wind shell. Conditions change fast.
Connectivity — Cell coverage is patchy to nonexistent for most of the drive. Download offline maps before you leave.
Wildlife — Bighorn sheep, mountain goats, bears. Pull fully off the road for wildlife and never feed them. Slow down at dusk.
