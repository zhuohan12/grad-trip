Porteau Cove lookout and shore

Required stop Short walk

Use the signed provincial-park entrance and day-use lot. Walk the short path toward the pier and shoreline for the open Howe Sound view; if everyone is moving efficiently, add the park's 300 m lookout trail. Do not substitute roadside parking if the lot is full.

Majestic boulder

Required stop View only

The exact pin is Majestic, a V6 deep-water boulder above Howe Sound. Park only at the legal northbound pullout near 49.6060, −123.2161 and follow the informal ground approach; do not use the railway-tunnel route. This schedule allows a look and photos at the boulder, not a climbing session. The predicted Squamish Inner tide is rising after a 6:26 AM low, but cold water, fall consequences, and rescue logistics still make an improvised attempt inappropriate.

Shannon Falls viewpoint

Required stop Easy 350 m trail

Take the easy forest path from the day-use lot to the lower viewing platform for the 335 m waterfall. Keep this to the viewpoint rather than continuing toward the Chief. Stay on the trail and platform: BC Parks warns that the creek edges and rocks are extremely slippery and high flow can carry debris.

Falls viewpoint  ·  ~35 min  ·  Easy

Green Lake Pullout

Worthwhile lookout

Pass through Whistler and stop at the northbound pullout about 6 km beyond the village. The glacial-green lake and surrounding peaks are visible directly from the turnout, making this a better transfer-day pause than finding village parking. Stay behind the barrier and away from the unstable bluff edge.

Roadside pull-off  ·  ~10 min

Pemberton fuel and lunch

Last services

Refuel, use the toilets, and take away lunch before Mount Currie. Download the offline route and check DriveBC one more time while there is reliable service. The next roughly 100 km to Lillooet has no dependable fuel, food, mobile service, rest areas, or EV charging, plus steep grades and tight bends.

Fuel + takeaway lunch  ·  ~40 min
5:05 PM

Stl’akw / Duffey Lake Viewpoint

Worthwhile lookout

Use the formal gravel viewpoint parking at the east end of Duffey Lake for the best no-hike alpine stop of the day. This is a short photo pause only; there is no potable water, and wind can rise quickly around the lake.

Roadside viewpoint  ·  ~15 min
6:20 PM

Seton Lake Lookout

Best interior view Short steep trail

Park at the large Highway 99 pullout just west of the final switchback and take the short trail to the elevated view over Seton Lake and the curling road. Budget about 1 km return and 80 m of gain. The trailhead is across the highway from parking: skip this stop if traffic, heat, smoke, or the crossing feels uncomfortable.

Continue north from Lillooet

Stay on Highway 99 north through Pavilion and Marble Canyon to the Highway 97 junction near Cache Creek, then take Highway 1 east to Kamloops. Do not let navigation reroute the trip south through Lytton. Add only a fuel or bathroom stop; the remaining drive is about 2½ hours.

Check in at DoubleTree Kamloops

Day complete

Finish at 339 St Paul Street after roughly 2½ hours from Lillooet. Keep the evening simple: check in, eat nearby, refill water, and reset the car for the 7:00 AM departure toward Jasper the next morning.

Field notes & logistics

Rental pickup — The Canada Trip 2026 booking starts at 9:00 AM at 970 SW Marine Drive #10. The road schedule assumes a one-hour handover and a 10:00 AM departure.

Road status — Check DriveBC before leaving and again in Pemberton. Highway 99 can close with little notice after crashes, wildfire activity, rockfall, or debris flow.

Majestic — The itinerary uses the legal pullout/ground approach described by current climbing access notes. Never use the railway tunnel. A climb is outside today's time and safety budget.

Tide — The official Squamish Inner prediction for July 21 is low at 6:26 AM, then high at 1:03 PM. Treat it as context, not a safety guarantee.

Remote section — The Mount Currie–Lillooet corridor has steep grades up to 15%, tight 20 km/h curves, single-lane bridges, no passing lanes, and long gaps without mobile service. Use lower gears on descents.

Food and fuel — Buy water, breakfast, and road snacks before pickup, then top up the tank and take away a late lunch in Pemberton. Do not rely on finding dinner before the late Kamloops arrival.

Schedule — All planned stops produce an arrival around 9:35 PM. Keep Porteau Cove, Majestic, and Shannon Falls; if pickup or traffic slips by more than 45 minutes, cut Seton Lake first.
