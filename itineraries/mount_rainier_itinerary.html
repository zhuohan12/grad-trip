Depart South Seattle
~2 hrs to the Nisqually entrance via I-5 south → SR-512 → SR-7 → SR-706. Fill up on petrol before SR-7 — gas stations thin out past Elbe. Pack lunch, water (3L+ per person), sunscreen, layers. The mountain is a 14,411-ft weather generator; pack a wind shell even if Seattle is clear.
Paradise · Subalpine wildflower meadows
7:00 AM
Paradise · Skyline Trail
Main hike Timed entry
Early August is peak wildflower bloom at 5,400 ft — lupine, paintbrush, avalanche lily, and pasqueflower in full colour across the meadows. The Skyline Loop climbs to Panorama Point with the Tatoosh Range to the south and Rainier's south face filling the sky north. Counter-clockwise direction is the classic — saves the steepest climb for fresher legs.
Skyline Trail loop  5.5 mi  ·  1,700 ft gain  ·  ~3.5 hrs  ·  Moderate–strenuous
11:30 AM
Lunch + drive to Sunrise
Quick lunch at the Paradise Inn or in the car. Drive east on Stevens Canyon Road → Hwy 123 north → Hwy 410 north to the Sunrise turnoff. ~1.5–2 hrs depending on construction and traffic. Stevens Canyon has multiple worthwhile pull-offs (Reflection Lakes, Box Canyon) — skip them today, save for a future trip.
Sunrise · Alpine ridges + glacier views
2:00 PM
Sunrise · Mount Fremont Fire Lookout
Main hike Timed entry
Sunrise sits at 6,400 ft — the highest point in the park you can drive to. The lookout trail climbs through Sourdough Ridge wildflowers to a 1930s fire lookout perched at 7,317 ft with a panoramic view of Rainier's north face, the Emmons Glacier (largest glacier by area in the lower 48), and on a clear day, Mount Baker. Mountain goats and marmots common. Ridgeline is fully exposed — sun protection essential.
Mount Fremont Lookout Trail  5.6 mi RT  ·  900 ft gain  ·  ~3 hrs  ·  Moderate
5:30 PM
Golden hour at Sunrise
Linger 30 min at the Sunrise Visitor Center deck or wander the easy 1.5 mi Sunrise Rim Trail for last light on the Emmons Glacier. August sunset is around 8:35 PM but you'll want to be back on the road by 6 PM to reach Seattle by 9 PM.
6:00 PM
Drive home
Hwy 410 west through Enumclaw, then SR-164 → SR-18 → I-5 north back to South Seattle. ~2.5 hrs. Dinner stop in Enumclaw if you didn't pack one.
Be honest with yourself about this day.  Two separate hikes totalling 11 miles and 2,600 ft of gain, bracketed by 4.5 hrs of driving. It's doable but punishing. If you'd rather not start at 5 AM: drop Mount Fremont and just do Paradise + Skyline, returning via the same route — saves ~3 hrs. Or split into two days with an overnight in Ashford or Packwood between Paradise and Sunrise.
Field notes & logistics
Timed entry permits — Required summer 2026 for both Paradise and Sunrise corridors. Book at recreation.gov 60 days in advance, or 7 days ahead for next-day slots. $2 reservation fee per vehicle.
Entry pass — America the Beautiful pass ($80/yr) covers MORA + all US national parks. Or $30 vehicle pass at the gate.
Sunrise Road dates — Typically opens late June, closes mid-October. Confirm at nps.gov/mora before the trip.
Wildflower timing — Early August is peak Paradise bloom; Sunrise blooms 1–2 weeks later. You're hitting both at strong-to-peak.
Weather — Rainier makes its own clouds. Check the NWS Mount Rainier Recreation Forecast morning-of. Bring a wind shell + warm layer even if Seattle is hot.
Altitude — Sunrise is 6,400 ft, Mount Fremont 7,317 ft. Not high enough for sickness but expect to feel mildly winded. Hydrate aggressively.
Gear — Sun hat, SPF 50+, sunglasses (snow + glacier glare), 3L water, trekking poles for Skyline descent, layers.
Wildlife — Marmots, mountain goats, black bear. Goats can be aggressive — keep 50 yds. Don't feed marmots; they're salt-conditioned to backpacks.
