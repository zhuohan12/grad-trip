01
Spokane to Tacoma, over Chinook Pass to Sunrise
Saturday, Aug 1 · Long scenic drive + an alpine ridge hike
6:00 AM
Depart Spokane
It's a big driving day — ~290 mi / ~5.5 hrs to Sunrise — so get rolling early. Take I-90 west across the Columbia Plateau to Ellensburg (~2.5 hrs), then drop toward Yakima and pick up SR-410, the Chinook Pass Highway. Both Spokane and Tacoma are on Pacific time, so no clock change today. Fuel up in Ellensburg or Naches; there's none in the park, and Packwood is the nearest pump to Sunrise. Pack lunch, 3L+ water each, sunscreen and a wind shell — the mountain makes its own weather at 14,411 ft.
Chinook Pass · the eastern gateway
~10:30 AM
Chinook Pass & Tipsoo Lake
Seasonal road — open summer only
SR-410 climbs to 5,430 ft at Chinook Pass, cresting right at the park's east boundary. Tipsoo Lake sits just below — a subalpine tarn that mirrors Rainier, ringed by wildflowers in early August. It's the perfect leg-stretch and the first knockout view of the peak. Chinook Pass closes all winter and reopened for the season on May 22, 2026, so it's well open now; still worth a glance at the WSDOT pass report the night before.
Sunrise · Alpine ridges + glacier views
~11:30 AM
Sunrise · Mount Fremont Fire Lookout
Main hike
From the pass it's ~45 min down and around to the White River Entrance and up the Sunrise Road to 6,400 ft — the highest point in the park you can drive to. Eat a quick lunch at the visitor center, then take the lookout trail through Sourdough Ridge wildflowers to a 1930s fire lookout at 7,317 ft: a panorama of Rainier's north face, the Emmons Glacier (largest by area in the lower 48), and Mount Baker on a clear day. Marmots and mountain goats are common; the ridgeline is fully exposed, so sun protection is essential. Short on energy after the drive? The easy Sourdough Ridge nature trail or the 1.5 mi Sunrise Rim loop give the same views for a fraction of the effort.
Mount Fremont Lookout Trail  5.6 mi RT  ·  900 ft gain  ·  ~3 hrs  ·  Moderate
~4:00 PM
Drive down to Tacoma
Back down the Sunrise Road to SR-410 and out the northwest side through Greenwater and Enumclaw, then SR-410 → SR-167 / SR-512 into Tacoma. ~2 to 2.5 hrs. Check in at 3918 North Mullen Street — your base for the next two nights — and grab dinner in Tacoma's Proctor or Sixth Ave district. Tomorrow's Paradise start is much gentler than the old 5 AM plan.
Sunrise → Tacoma  ~90 mi  ·  ~2–2.5 hrs
02
Paradise & the Skyline Trail, then a Seattle evening
Sunday, Aug 2 · Wildflower meadows + city dinner
6:30 AM
Depart Tacoma for the Nisqually entrance
~1.5 hrs to Paradise via SR-7 through Elbe, then SR-706 to the Nisqually Entrance and up to the Paradise lot. Leave early — Nisqually is the busiest gate and the entry line and Paradise parking both fill by mid-morning in August. Fill up before Elbe; the gas station outside Ashford is the last one. Pack the same layers and water as yesterday.
Paradise · Subalpine wildflower meadows
~8:00 AM
Paradise · Skyline Trail loop
Main hike
Early August is peak bloom at 5,400 ft — lupine, paintbrush, avalanche lily and pasqueflower across the meadows. The Skyline Loop climbs to Panorama Point with the Tatoosh Range to the south and Rainier's south face filling the sky to the north. Go counter-clockwise to save the steepest pitch for fresher legs, and start early for cool air, parking, and the meadows to yourself before the crowds.
Skyline Trail loop  5.5 mi  ·  1,700 ft gain  ·  ~3.5 hrs  ·  Moderate–strenuous
~12:30 PM
Lunch + optional Paradise wander
Lunch at the Paradise Inn or a picnic below the meadows. If anyone wants more, the short Nisqually Vista or Myrtle Falls strolls are gentle add-ons. Then start north — you're trading the mountain for the city this afternoon.
Down the mountain · up to Seattle
~5:00 PM
Seattle · dinner in the city
City evening
~2.5 hrs from Paradise back down SR-706 → SR-7 → I-5 north (you'll pass Tacoma on the way up). Aim for an early evening in Seattle: Pike Place Market, the waterfront, and dinner downtown or in Belltown. Cap it with the view from Kerry Park across to the skyline and Rainier if it's out. A relaxed counterpoint to two hard hiking days.
~9:00 PM
Back to Tacoma
~40 min south on I-5 to the Mullen Street base for the second night. Keeping the room in Tacoma means no packing tonight — an easy end to the day.
03
Seattle morning, then the ferry to the peninsula
Monday, Aug 3 · City half-day + Bainbridge crossing to Port Angeles
8:30 AM
Check out, drive up to Seattle
Load the car and head ~40 min north on I-5 into Seattle. Today is deliberately light — a half-day in the city, then a scenic ferry west to start the Olympic leg by evening.
Seattle · a half-day in the city
~9:30 AM
Morning in Seattle
City time
Pick a couple of highlights rather than rushing all of them: Pike Place Market at its liveliest in the morning, the waterfront and the Seattle Aquarium, the Chihuly Garden and Glass or Space Needle at Seattle Center, or coffee and a wander through Pioneer Square. Grab an early lunch so you're at the ferry terminal with time to spare.
Puget Sound · the crossing west
~1:30 PM
Seattle → Bainbridge Island ferry
Car ferry Arrive early to load
Drive onto the Washington State Ferry at Colman Dock (Pier 52) for the 35-minute sailing to Bainbridge Island — one of the best cheap views in the country, with the skyline shrinking behind you and the Olympics rising ahead. Fares are collected westbound only. Summer sailings are frequent but fill with vehicles, so join the car queue ~45–60 min ahead; check current times at wsdot.wa.gov/ferries.
Colman Dock → Bainbridge  ~35 min sailing
~4:30 PM
Bainbridge → Port Angeles
Off the boat, it's ~2 hrs west via SR-305 → SR-3 → SR-104 across the Hood Canal Bridge, then US-101 along the strait into Port Angeles — your base for all three Olympic nights. Arrive early evening, drop the bags, and grab dinner on Front Street. The Olympic leg picks up from here, with Hurricane Ridge stargazing an option tonight if the road's open.
Bainbridge → Port Angeles  ~70 mi  ·  ~2 hrs
The load is the driving, not the hiking now.  Splitting Rainier across two mornings — Sunrise on Aug 1, Paradise on Aug 2 — means each hike is a manageable ~5.5 mi rather than the old back-to-back 11-mile day. The catch is Aug 1's ~5.5 hr drive from Spokane before you hike, so leave by 6 AM, keep the Sunrise hike flexible (Mount Fremont if fresh, the Sourdough/Rim loops if not), and don't stack a big evening on top. Aug 2 and 3 are easy by comparison. One thing to lock in: Aug 3's westbound ferry fills with cars in summer, so build in the 45–60 min car-queue buffer or you'll wait for the next sailing and reach Port Angeles late.
Field notes & logistics
Timed entry (2026) — Mount Rainier is not running timed-entry reservations in 2026, so no advance permit is needed. The trade-off is long entry lines: expect 60+ min waits at Nisqually and 30–60 min for Sunrise between roughly 8 AM and 4 PM in July–August. Both of our starts are early to beat them. Always re-check nps.gov/mora before the trip in case the policy changes.
Entry pass — America the Beautiful pass ($80/yr) covers MORA + all US national parks. Or $35 vehicle pass at the gate (card only on-site; fastest to pre-pay online).
Chinook Pass (SR-410) — The scenic eastern approach from Spokane, closed all winter and reopened May 22, 2026 for the season. Confirm it's open on the WSDOT mountain pass report and the NPS road-status page the night before.
Sunrise Road dates — Typically opens early July, closes early–mid October (highest road in the park, so snow lingers). Confirm at nps.gov/mora before the trip.
Bainbridge ferry (Aug 3) — Washington State Ferries, Seattle Colman Dock → Bainbridge, ~35 min. Fares collected westbound only; vehicles fill in summer, so join the car queue 45–60 min early. Live times at wsdot.wa.gov/ferries.
Wildflower timing — Early August is peak Paradise bloom; Sunrise blooms 1–2 weeks later. You're hitting both at strong-to-peak.
Weather — Rainier makes its own clouds. Check the NWS Mount Rainier Recreation Forecast morning-of. Bring a wind shell + warm layer even if the lowlands are hot.
Altitude — Sunrise is 6,400 ft, Mount Fremont 7,317 ft. Not high enough for sickness but expect to feel mildly winded. Hydrate aggressively.
Gear & wildlife — Sun hat, SPF 50+, sunglasses, 3L water, trekking poles for the Skyline descent, layers. Marmots, mountain goats and black bear about — keep 50 yds from goats and never feed the marmots.
